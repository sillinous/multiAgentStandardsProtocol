# SuperStandard Consortium Intellectual Property Rights Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** TBD  
**Status:** Draft for Founding Members

## Executive Summary

This Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) Policy governs all intellectual property contributed to, created by, or incorporated into SuperStandard Consortium specifications and reference implementations. The policy is designed to:

- **Ensure Freedom to Implement**: Anyone can implement specifications without patent concerns
- **Promote Adoption**: Royalty-free licensing encourages widespread use
- **Protect Contributors**: Clear rules about ownership and rights
- **Enable Innovation**: Open licensing supports ecosystem growth

**Core Principle:** All SuperStandard specifications shall be implementable on a **Royalty-Free (RF)** basis.

## 1. Scope and Application

### 1.1 Covered Materials

This policy applies to:

- **Specifications**: All protocol specifications, technical standards, and related documentation
- **Reference Implementations**: Official code examples and reference software
- **Test Suites**: Compliance testing tools and test cases
- **Documentation**: Technical guides, tutorials, and examples
- **Contributions**: All technical contributions to working groups

### 1.2 Who Must Comply

- All Consortium members (all tiers)
- All technical contributors to working groups
- All participants in specification development
- All users of "SuperStandard Certified" marks

### 1.3 Effective Date

- Applies to all work from date of joining/contributing
- No retroactive application to work done before joining
- Survives termination of membership

**Licensing:**

## 3. Patent Policy

### 3.1 Patent Licensing Commitment

**Royalty-Free (RF) Mode:**

All members and contributors commit to license any **Essential Claims** on a **Royalty-Free** basis to anyone implementing SuperStandard specifications.

**Essential Claims Definition:**
Patent claims that would necessarily be infringed by implementation of a required (normative) portion of a specification, where no commercially reasonable alternative exists.

### 3.2 Licensing Terms

**RF License Terms:**

1. **Scope**: Covers implementation of SuperStandard specification only
2. **Royalty**: No royalties or fees
3. **Duration**: Perpetual and irrevocable
4. **Geographic**: Worldwide
5. **Sublicensing**: Fully sublicensable
6. **Fields of Use**: All fields of use
7. **Reciprocity**: Subject to reciprocal licensing (see below)

### 3.3 Reciprocal Licensing Condition

The RF license is conditioned upon reciprocity:

- Licensee must also grant RF license on their Essential Claims
- Applies only to same specification
- Defensive: If licensee sues for patent infringement related to specification, their license may terminate

### 3.4 Patent Exclusion Mechanism

**Before Specification Approval:**

Contributors may exclude specific patents by:
1. Identifying specific patent(s) or application(s)
2. Filing written exclusion with TSC
3. Deadline: 90 days after Last Call for specification
4. Public disclosure required

**Effect of Exclusion:**
- Excluded claims not subject to RF licensing
- Working group may redesign specification to avoid excluded patents
- If specification not redesigned, excluded patent holder may not claim compliance

**No Exclusion After Approval:**
- After specification approved, no exclusions permitted
- RF licensing commitment becomes binding and irrevocable

### 3.5 Member Patent Obligations

**Upon Joining:**
- Member discloses known Essential Claims
- Member commits to RF licensing per this policy
- Commitment applies to member's affiliates

**During Participation:**
- Ongoing duty to disclose known Essential Claims
- Disclosure in good faith
- Templates provided for disclosure

**Upon Withdrawal:**
- RF licensing commitments survive membership termination
- Applies to specifications worked on during membership
- No new obligations for specifications after withdrawal

## 4. Trademark Policy

### 4.1 Consortium Trademarks

**Owned Marks:**
- "SuperStandard" (word mark)
- "SuperStandard Consortium" (word mark)
- SuperStandard logos and design marks
- "SuperStandard Certified" certification marks
- Protocol names (e.g., "BAP", "ANP", "ACP")

**Ownership:** SuperStandard Consortium (or designated legal entity)

### 4.2 Member Use of Marks

**Permitted Uses (All Members):**

✓ Factual references to membership status
- "Proud member of SuperStandard Consortium"
- "Contributing to SuperStandard protocols"

✓ Factual statements about implementations
- "Implements SuperStandard ANP v1.0"
- "Compatible with SuperStandard protocols"

✓ Use of membership tier badges
- "Platinum Member" badge on website
- Logo in marketing materials per brand guidelines

**Prohibited Uses:**

✗ Implying endorsement without certification
✗ Using "SuperStandard Certified" without certification
✗ Modifying consortium logos or marks
✗ Using marks in company name without permission
✗ Domain names including "superstandard" (except with permission)

### 4.3 Certification Mark Usage

**"SuperStandard Certified" Mark:**

- Only for products/services that pass certification
- License agreement required
- Subject to compliance monitoring
- Revocable if compliance lost

**Permitted Claims:**
- "SuperStandard Certified - Gold Level"
- Display certification badge with tier
- Use in marketing and sales materials

**Requirements:**
- Maintain valid certification
- Follow brand guidelines
- Annual compliance verification
- Report compliance issues

### 4.4 Brand Guidelines

**Technical Requirements:**
- Use official logo files (provided)
- Maintain minimum clear space
- Don't alter colors or proportions
- Don't use as part of product name

**Quality Control:**
- Consortium may review trademark usage
- Request corrections for misuse
- Revoke license for persistent violations

## 5. Contribution Licensing

### 5.1 Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

**Required From:**
- All individuals contributing to specifications
- All individuals contributing code
- All individuals participating in working groups

**Two Forms:**
1. **Individual CLA**: For individual contributors
2. **Corporate CLA**: For employees of member companies

### 5.2 Individual CLA Terms

**Grants:**

1. **Copyright License**
   - License contributions under CC BY 4.0 (specs) or Apache 2.0 (code)
   - Irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free
   - Right to sublicense

2. **Patent License**
   - RF license on Essential Claims in contributions
   - Defensive termination clause
   - Scope limited to specific contribution

**Representations:**
- Contributor has rights to make grants
- Contribution is original work or properly licensed
- No known conflicting obligations

**Sign Once:**
- CLA applies to all future contributions
- Maintained in public registry
- Electronic signature accepted

### 5.3 Corporate CLA Terms

**Scope:**
- Covers all employees of company
- Company responsible for employee compliance
- Company maintains list of covered employees

**Grants:**
- Same as Individual CLA
- Company grants on behalf of employees
- Survives employment termination

**Benefits:**
- Employees don't sign individual CLAs
- Streamlined contribution process
- Clear corporate approval

### 5.4 Contribution Process

**Steps:**
1. Sign CLA (if not already signed)
2. Make contribution (GitHub PR, specification edit, etc.)
3. Affirm CLA compliance in contribution
4. Working group reviews technical merit
5. If approved, incorporated into work product

**Automated Checking:**
- CLA status checked automatically on GitHub
- Contributors notified if CLA needed
- No contributions accepted without CLA

## 6. Reference Implementation Licensing

### 6.1 Apache 2.0 License

All official reference implementations use **Apache License 2.0**.

**Why Apache 2.0:**
- Well-understood, widely-adopted license
- Includes explicit patent grant
- Permissive (compatible with commercial use)
- GPLv3 compatible
- Includes trademark protection

### 6.2 Reference Implementation Policy

**Official Implementations:**
- Maintained by Consortium or working groups
- Licensed under Apache 2.0
- Copyright held by contributors
- Attribution required

**Community Implementations:**
- Not required to use Apache 2.0
- Encouraged to use OSI-approved licenses
- Can be proprietary
- Cannot claim "official" status without approval

### 6.3 Third-Party Dependencies

**Permitted:**
- Apache 2.0 licensed dependencies
- MIT, BSD, ISC licenses
- Other permissive OSI-approved licenses

**Not Permitted:**
- GPL/LGPL (copyleft) dependencies in official implementations
- Proprietary dependencies
- Non-commercial licenses

**Review Required:**
- All dependencies reviewed by TSC
- License compatibility verified
- Security and quality checks

## 7. Disclosure Requirements

### 7.1 Patent Disclosure

**When Required:**
- Upon joining Consortium
- Upon joining working group
- When patent issued/discovered
- Before specification approval

**What to Disclose:**
- Patent number or application number
- Brief description of claims
- Which specification potentially affected
- Whether exclusion sought

**How to Disclose:**
- Submit to ipr@superstandard.org
- Use disclosure form template
- Public disclosure (posted on website)
- Confidential review available (limited scope)

### 7.2 Licensing Terms Disclosure

If member seeks to exclude patent:
- Disclose licensing terms willing to offer
- Include royalty rates if RAND
- Specify any restrictions or conditions
- Timeline for negotiation

### 7.3 Third-Party IP

**Must Disclose:**
- Known third-party patents affecting specifications
- Licensing agreements that impact contributions
- Restrictions on contributions

**Good Faith:**
- No obligation to conduct patent search
- Disclose what is actually known
- Update if new information discovered

## 8. Exclusions and Limitations

### 8.1 What Is NOT Licensed

**Trademarks:**
- Patent/copyright license does NOT include trademark license
- Separate certification required for "SuperStandard Certified" mark
- Company trademarks remain owned by companies

**Implementation-Specific IP:**
- Optimizations not required by specification
- Proprietary extensions beyond specification
- Trade secrets and know-how
- User interface designs

**Non-Essential Patents:**
- Patents not necessarily infringed by spec implementation
- Alternative implementations exist
- Optional (non-normative) portions of specification

### 8.2 No Warranty

**Disclaimer:**
- Specifications provided "AS IS"
- No warranty of non-infringement
- No warranty of fitness for purpose
- Implementation at own risk

**No Patent Search:**
- Consortium does not conduct patent searches
- No guarantee specifications are unencumbered
- Members responsible for own patent clearance

### 8.3 Liability Limitation

**No Liability:**
- Consortium not liable for patent infringement
- Members not liable to each other
- Each implementer responsible for own due diligence

**Indemnification:**
- No cross-indemnification between members
- Each member responsible for own contributions

## 9. Compliance and Enforcement

### 9.1 Monitoring

**Consortium May:**
- Request disclosure updates annually
- Review member patent portfolios (public information)
- Investigate potential violations
- Audit certification mark usage

**Members Must:**
- Respond to disclosure requests
- Report policy violations discovered
- Cooperate with investigations

### 9.2 Violation Remedies

**Process:**
1. Written notice of alleged violation
2. 30-day cure period
3. If not cured, escalate to Governing Board
4. Governing Board determines remedy

**Possible Remedies:**
- Require corrective action
- Suspend membership benefits
- Revoke certification marks
- Terminate membership
- Legal action (last resort)

### 9.3 Dispute Resolution

**Steps:**
1. Good faith negotiation (30 days)
2. Mediation (60 days)
3. Arbitration (binding)
4. Litigation (only if arbitration fails)

**Venue:**
- Arbitration in San Francisco, CA
- Rules: AAA Commercial Arbitration Rules
- One arbitrator
- Each party bears own costs

## 10. Definitions

**Affiliate:** Any entity controlling, controlled by, or under common control with a member.

**Contribution:** Any technical content submitted for inclusion in Consortium work products.

**Essential Claims:** Patent claims necessarily infringed by implementing required portions of specification.

**Implementer:** Any person or organization implementing a SuperStandard specification.

**Member:** Organization or individual with executed membership agreement.

**Normative:** Required elements of specification (versus informative/optional elements).

**Royalty-Free (RF):** No monetary payment required for patent license.

**Specification:** Formal technical standard developed and approved by Consortium.

**Working Group:** Technical group developing specifications under TSC charter.

## Appendix C: FAQ

**Q: Do I need a patent attorney to participate?**  
A: No, but understand that participating means RF licensing commitment.

**Q: Can I use specifications in commercial products?**  
A: Yes! That's the goal. Royalty-free means no fees for implementation.

**Q: What if my employer has patents?**  
A: Your employer should sign Corporate CLA to cover you.

**Q: Can I exclude patents I don't want to license?**  
A: Yes, before specification approval (90-day deadline after Last Call).

**Q: What happens if I leave my company?**  
A: Corporate CLA survives. Your individual contributions remain licensed.

**Q: Can I use SuperStandard logo?**  
A: Factual references OK. Certification marks require certification.

**Q: Are there any fees for patent licenses?**  
A: No! Royalty-Free means no fees, ever.

**Q: What if someone sues me for patent infringement?**  
A: RF license includes defensive termination for the suing party.

**Q: Can I create proprietary extensions?**  
A: Yes, but they're not covered by "certified" claims.

**Q: What license for my own implementation?**  
A: Your choice! Apache 2.0, MIT, proprietary - your decision.
