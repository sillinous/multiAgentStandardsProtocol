### Handling Objections

**Objection 1: "Why not just use existing standards like MCP?"**
- **Response:** MCP is great for one piece (model context). SuperStandard covers the full stack: discovery, coordination, economics, infrastructure. We're complementary, not competitive. In fact, we integrate MCP as one of our protocols.

**Objection 2: "We're already building our own agent platform."**
- **Response:** Perfect! SuperStandard gives you a standards-based foundation. You can differentiate on implementation while ensuring interoperability. Plus, your platform can become the reference implementation, positioning you as a leader.

**Objection 3: "Standards take too long to develop."**
- **Response:** We've already built 8 protocols with production implementations. This isn't starting from scratch‚Äîwe're consortium-izing proven technology. You're joining to govern and extend, not waiting years for specs to be written.

**Objection 4: "What if my competitors join?"**
- **Response:** They probably will, which is the point of open standards. But you'll be at the table influencing decisions. Better to shape standards WITH competitors than be locked out. Plus, standards grow the pie for everyone.

**Objection 5: "Is there really demand for this?"**
- **Response:** Every major tech shift required standards: Web (HTTP/HTML), Mobile (iOS/Android standards), Cloud (S3 API became de facto standard). Multi-agent systems are following the same pattern. Be early or be late‚Äîyour choice.

**Objection 6: "We don't have budget this year."**
- **Response:** Founding member discount is only available to first 20 members. At 50% off, you're saving $[X]K. Plus, the free certifications alone are worth $[X]K. Can you afford NOT to be at the table when standards are set?
