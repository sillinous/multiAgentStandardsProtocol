## BONUS 3: Governance Charter (Bylaws) Draft

### BYLAWS OF SUPERSTANDARD CONSORTIUM

**Adopted:** [Date]  
**Effective:** [Date]  
**Last Amended:** [Date]

---

### ARTICLE I - NAME AND PURPOSE

#### Section 1.1 Name
The name of this organization shall be "SuperStandard Consortium" (the "Consortium").

#### Section 1.2 Purpose
The Consortium is organized for the purpose of developing, maintaining, and 
promoting open, vendor-neutral, royalty-free technical standards and protocols 
for multi-agent artificial intelligence systems.

#### Section 1.3 Non-Profit Status
The Consortium shall operate as a [501(c)(6) non-profit trade association / 
limited liability company / project of Linux Foundation] under the laws of 
[State/Jurisdiction].

#### Section 1.4 Duration
The Consortium shall have perpetual existence unless dissolved as provided 
in Article XII.

---

### ARTICLE II - MEMBERSHIP

#### Section 2.1 Classes of Membership
The Consortium shall have five (5) classes of membership:
a) Platinum Members
b) Gold Members
c) Silver Members
d) Academic Members
e) Individual Members

#### Section 2.2 Platinum Membership
a) **Eligibility:** Any organization may apply for Platinum membership.
b) **Dues:** Annual dues of $50,000, as may be adjusted pursuant to Section 2.7.
c) **Rights:** 
   - One (1) seat on the Governing Board
   - Full voting rights on business and technical matters
   - Five (5) complimentary compliance certifications annually
   - All benefits enumerated in the Membership Tiers document
d) **Obligations:**
   - Execute and comply with Membership Agreement
   - Execute and comply with Intellectual Property Rights Policy
   - Pay dues annually in advance
   - Designate Board representative

#### Section 2.3 Gold Membership
a) **Eligibility:** Any organization may apply for Gold membership.
b) **Dues:** Annual dues of $25,000, as may be adjusted pursuant to Section 2.7.
c) **Rights:**
   - Eligible to vote for and serve as Gold Member class representatives to 
     Governing Board (three seats total for class)
   - Full voting rights on technical matters
   - Voting rights on business matters through elected representatives
   - Three (3) complimentary compliance certifications annually
   - All benefits enumerated in the Membership Tiers document
d) **Obligations:** Same as Platinum Members

#### Section 2.4 Silver Membership
a) **Eligibility:** Any organization may apply for Silver membership.
b) **Dues:** Annual dues of $10,000, as may be adjusted pursuant to Section 2.7.
c) **Rights:**
   - Eligible to vote for and serve as Silver Member class representatives to 
     Governing Board (two seats total for class)
   - Full voting rights on technical matters
   - Voting rights on business matters through elected representatives
   - One (1) complimentary compliance certification annually (Bronze or Silver tier)
   - All benefits enumerated in the Membership Tiers document
d) **Obligations:** Same as Platinum Members

#### Section 2.5 Academic Membership
a) **Eligibility:** Accredited universities, colleges, non-profit research 
   institutions, and government research laboratories.
b) **Dues:** None
c) **Rights:**
   - Observer status at Governing Board meetings
   - Full voting rights on technical matters
   - No voting rights on business matters
   - Complimentary compliance certification for research/educational use only
   - All benefits enumerated in the Membership Tiers document
d) **Obligations:**
   - Execute Academic Membership Agreement
   - Verify academic status annually
   - Use limited to non-commercial, research, and educational purposes
   - Comply with Intellectual Property Rights Policy

#### Section 2.6 Individual Membership
a) **Eligibility:** Any individual may apply for Individual membership.
b) **Dues:** None
c) **Rights:**
   - Observer status at Governing Board meetings
   - Full voting rights on technical matters
   - No voting rights on business matters
   - Complimentary Bronze certification for personal projects only
   - All benefits enumerated in the Membership Tiers document
d) **Obligations:**
   - Agree to Code of Conduct
   - Sign Contributor License Agreement for any technical contributions
   - Use of complimentary certifications limited to non-commercial purposes

#### Section 2.7 Dues Adjustment
a) The Governing Board may adjust membership dues annually by resolution.
b) Increases shall not exceed the lesser of:
   - Consumer Price Index (CPI) increase for the prior year, or
   - Five percent (5%)
c) Members shall receive ninety (90) days' notice of any dues increase.
d) Dues increases shall take effect at the start of the next membership year.

#### Section 2.8 Admission of Members
a) **Application:** Prospective members shall submit an application in the 
   form prescribed by the Executive Director.
b) **Review:** The Executive Director shall review applications for completeness 
   and eligibility.
c) **Approval:**
   - Platinum and Gold membership applications require Governing Board approval.
   - Silver, Academic, and Individual applications may be approved by the 
     Executive Director.
d) **Effective Date:** Membership becomes effective upon:
   - Execution of Membership Agreement
   - Payment of first year's dues (for paid tiers)
   - Acceptance by Consortium

#### Section 2.9 Membership Term and Renewal
a) **Term:** Membership is for a period of one (1) year from the effective date.
b) **Renewal:** Members may renew annually by:
   - Executing renewal agreement (or auto-renewal if elected)
   - Paying renewal dues (for paid tiers)
c) **Notice:** The Consortium shall provide sixty (60) days' notice before 
   membership expiration.
d) **Grace Period:** A thirty (30) day grace period shall apply after expiration.
e) **Lapse:** If not renewed within grace period, membership shall lapse.

#### Section 2.10 Suspension and Termination
a) **Voluntary Termination:** A member may terminate membership by providing 
   thirty (30) days' written notice. No refund of dues shall be provided.
b) **Termination for Non-Payment:** If dues remain unpaid for thirty (30) days 
   after grace period, membership may be terminated by the Executive Director.
c) **Termination for Cause:** The Governing Board may terminate membership for:
   - Material breach of Membership Agreement
   - Violation of Intellectual Property Rights Policy
   - Violation of Code of Conduct
   - Conduct harmful to the Consortium
d) **Procedure for Termination for Cause:**
   - Written notice of alleged violation
   - Thirty (30) day cure period
   - If not cured, hearing before Governing Board (optional)
   - Governing Board vote (two-thirds majority required)
e) **Effect of Termination:**
   - All membership rights cease immediately
   - No refund of dues
   - Intellectual Property Rights Policy obligations survive
   - Certification licenses may be revoked

#### Section 2.11 Transfers
Membership is non-transferable except:
a) In the event of merger or acquisition, subject to Governing Board approval
b) Transfer of Membership Agreement to successor entity
c) No transfer fee for approved transfers

### ARTICLE III - GOVERNING BOARD

#### Section 3.1 Powers and Duties
The Governing Board shall have general management and control of the business, 
property, and affairs of the Consortium, including:
a) Approve annual budget
b) Set membership dues and fee structures
c) Approve major policy changes
d) Hire, evaluate, and if necessary, terminate the Executive Director
e) Approve Intellectual Property Rights Policy and amendments
f) Approve dissolution of the Consortium
g) Exercise all powers of the Consortium except as otherwise provided in these Bylaws

#### Section 3.2 Composition
The Governing Board shall consist of:
a) **Platinum Member Representatives:** One (1) seat per Platinum Member organization
b) **Gold Member Representatives:** Three (3) seats elected by and from Gold Members
c) **Silver Member Representatives:** Two (2) seats elected by and from Silver Members
d) **Technical Steering Committee Representative:** One (1) seat (TSC Chair)
e) **Executive Director:** Ex-officio, non-voting

#### Section 3.3 Term
a) **Platinum Representatives:** Serve at the pleasure of their appointing organization
b) **Elected Representatives (Gold/Silver):** Two (2) year terms, renewable
c) **TSC Representative:** One (1) year term coinciding with TSC Chair term
d) **Staggered Terms:** Elections shall be staggered so approximately half of 
   elected seats are elected each year

#### Section 3.4 Elections
a) **Eligibility:** Any representative of a Gold or Silver member organization 
   may stand for election to their respective class seats.
b) **Nomination:** 
   - Self-nominations permitted
   - Nomination period: Thirty (30) days before election
c) **Voting:**
   - One vote per organization (not per individual)
   - Ranked-choice voting method
   - Electronic voting permitted
d) **Timeline:** Elections held annually in Q4 for seats expiring at year-end

#### Section 3.5 Vacancies
a) **Occurrence:** A vacancy occurs when a Board member:
   - Resigns
   - Is removed
   - Organization's membership terminates
   - Is unable to serve due to death or incapacity
b) **Filling Vacancies:**
   - Platinum seat: Appointing organization designates replacement immediately
   - Elected seat: Special election within sixty (60) days, or appointment by 
     Board until next regular election

#### Section 3.6 Removal
a) **Platinum Representatives:** May be removed by their appointing organization 
   at any time
b) **Elected Representatives:** May be removed by two-thirds (2/3) vote of their 
   member class for:
   - Failure to attend three (3) consecutive meetings without excuse
   - Breach of fiduciary duty
   - Conduct harmful to Consortium
c) **Procedure:** 
   - Written notice of grounds for removal
   - Opportunity to respond (written or in person)
   - Vote by member class

#### Section 3.7 Meetings
a) **Regular Meetings:** At least quarterly, on schedule set by Board
b) **Special Meetings:** May be called by:
   - Board Chair
   - Executive Director
   - Written request of three (3) or more Board members
c) **Notice:** At least seven (7) days for regular meetings; forty-eight (48) 
   hours for special meetings
d) **Location:** Meetings may be held in person, by telephone, video conference, 
   or other electronic means
e) **Open Meetings:** All members may attend as observers, except for executive 
   sessions

#### Section 3.8 Quorum and Voting
a) **Quorum:** A majority (50% + 1) of voting Board members constitutes a quorum
b) **Voting:**
   - **Simple Majority:** Most business decisions (>50% of votes cast)
   - **Supermajority:** Budget approval, bylaw amendments, IP policy changes, 
     dissolution (≥66% of votes cast)
   - **Unanimous:** Changes to membership voting rights (100% of votes cast)
c) **Proxy:** Written proxy voting permitted for Platinum and elected representatives 
   (valid for single meeting only)
d) **Electronic Voting:** Permitted between meetings for time-sensitive matters 
   (minimum 7-day voting period)

#### Section 3.9 Committees
The Board may establish committees as needed, including:
a) Executive Committee
b) Finance Committee
c) Audit Committee
d) Nominating Committee

#### Section 3.10 Officers
a) The Board shall elect the following officers from among its members:
   - Board Chair
   - Vice Chair
   - Treasurer
   - Secretary
b) Officers serve one (1) year terms, renewable
c) Officer elections held annually at first Board meeting of calendar year

### ARTICLE IV - TECHNICAL STEERING COMMITTEE

#### Section 4.1 Purpose
The Technical Steering Committee (TSC) shall provide technical leadership and 
oversee the development and approval of all technical specifications and standards.

#### Section 4.2 Composition
a) **Elected Members:** Five to nine (5-9) individuals elected by technical contributors
b) **Working Group Chairs:** Ex-officio, non-voting
c) **Executive Director:** Ex-officio, non-voting

#### Section 4.3 Eligibility
Any individual who has made substantial technical contributions to Consortium 
projects in the past twelve (12) months, regardless of employer affiliation, 
may stand for election or vote in TSC elections.

#### Section 4.4 Term and Elections
a) **Term:** Two (2) years, renewable once (maximum four consecutive years)
b) **Staggered Terms:** Approximately half of seats elected each year
c) **Election Method:** Ranked-choice voting
d) **Timeline:** Annual elections in Q1

#### Section 4.5 Powers and Duties
a) Approve technical specifications and protocol versions
b) Establish technical roadmap and priorities
c) Create and charter working groups
d) Approve working group deliverables
e) Resolve technical disputes and appeals
f) Maintain technical governance documents
g) Approve reference implementations
h) Set technical quality standards
i) Review and approve RFCs (Requests for Comments)

#### Section 4.6 Decision Making
a) **Consensus First:** Strive for rough consensus on all technical decisions
b) **Vote When Needed:** If consensus fails, simple majority (>50%)
c) **Quorum:** Majority (50% + 1) of voting TSC members
d) **Lazy Consensus:** For routine decisions, assume approval if no objections 
   within seven (7) days

#### Section 4.7 Meetings
a) **Regular Meetings:** Weekly video conferences (open to community observers)
b) **Monthly Reviews:** In-depth technical reviews
c) **Annual Face-to-Face:** At least one in-person meeting per year
d) **Open Participation:** All meetings open to observers; recordings published

### ARTICLE V - EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

#### Section 5.1 Appointment
The Governing Board shall hire the Executive Director, who shall serve at the 
pleasure of the Board.

#### Section 5.2 Duties
The Executive Director shall:
a) Execute Consortium strategy and operations
b) Manage staff and contractors
c) Execute approved budget
d) Recruit and support members
e) Oversee marketing and communications
f) Plan and execute events
g) Manage contracts and agreements
h) Represent Consortium publicly
i) Support all governing bodies
j) Perform other duties as assigned by Governing Board

#### Section 5.3 Reporting
The Executive Director shall report to the Governing Board and provide:
a) Monthly operational updates
b) Quarterly financial reports
c) Annual performance review
d) Strategic planning recommendations

#### Section 5.4 Compensation
Compensation shall be determined by the Governing Board and reviewed annually.

---

### ARTICLE VI - INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

#### Section 6.1 Intellectual Property Rights Policy
All members and contributors shall comply with the Consortium's Intellectual 
Property Rights Policy, as adopted by the Governing Board and amended from time to time.

#### Section 6.2 Royalty-Free Commitment
The Consortium is committed to Royalty-Free (RF) licensing for all technical 
specifications and standards.

#### Section 6.3 Licenses
a) **Specifications:** Licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International
b) **Reference Implementations:** Licensed under Apache License 2.0
c) **Trademarks:** Controlled by Consortium; licensed to certified implementers

#### Section 6.4 Contributor License Agreements
All technical contributors must execute a Contributor License Agreement (CLA) 
before contributing.

---

### ARTICLE VII - FINANCIAL PROVISIONS

#### Section 7.1 Fiscal Year
The fiscal year shall be the calendar year (January 1 - December 31).

#### Section 7.2 Budget
a) The Executive Director shall prepare an annual budget for Governing Board approval
b) Budget must be approved by supermajority (66%) vote
c) Approval must occur before start of fiscal year
d) Material amendments (>10% variance) require Board approval

#### Section 7.3 Financial Reports
a) Monthly financial statements to Executive Director
b) Quarterly financial reports to Governing Board
c) Annual financial statements (audited if revenue > $1 million)
d) Annual report to members (summary)

#### Section 7.4 Audit
a) The Board shall select an independent auditor when revenue exceeds $1 million
b) Annual audits shall be conducted and presented to Governing Board
c) Audit results shall be summarized in annual report to members

#### Section 7.5 Reserves
a) The Consortium shall maintain minimum reserves of three (3) months operating expenses
b) Target reserves of six (6) months by end of Year 3
c) Excess reserves may be invested conservatively per Board-approved investment policy

#### Section 7.6 Contracts and Obligations
a) Contracts over $10,000 require Executive Director approval
b) Contracts over $50,000 require Governing Board approval
c) Competitive bidding required for contracts over $25,000

---

### ARTICLE VIII - INDEMNIFICATION

#### Section 8.1 Indemnification of Directors and Officers
The Consortium shall indemnify its directors, officers, and agents to the fullest 
extent permitted by law against expenses, liabilities, and claims arising from 
their service to the Consortium.

#### Section 8.2 Insurance
The Consortium shall maintain directors and officers (D&O) liability insurance 
and other appropriate insurance policies.

---

### ARTICLE IX - CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

#### Section 9.1 Disclosure
All Governing Board members, TSC members, and officers shall disclose any conflicts 
of interest annually and when they arise.

#### Section 9.2 Conflicts Register
A public register of disclosed conflicts shall be maintained.

#### Section 9.3 Recusal
Individuals with conflicts shall recuse themselves from related decisions.

#### Section 9.4 Vendor Control Prohibition
No single company may control more than 50% of any governing body.

---

### ARTICLE X - AMENDMENTS

#### Section 10.1 Process
These Bylaws may be amended by:
a) Proposal submitted to Governing Board
b) Thirty (30) day community comment period
c) Technical Steering Committee review (for technical governance changes)
d) Governing Board vote (supermajority required - 66%)
e) Thirty (30) day notice before effective date

#### Section 10.2 Version Control
All amendments shall be documented with:
a) Version number (semantic versioning)
b) Effective date
c) Change log
d) Rationale

### ARTICLE XI - DISSOLUTION

#### Section 11.1 Procedure

#### Section 11.2 Distribution of Assets
Upon dissolution:
a) Pay all debts and obligations
b) Distribute remaining assets to:
   - Similar 501(c)(6) organizations, or
   - Open source foundation (e.g., Linux Foundation), or
   - As directed by Governing Board, consistent with non-profit status

a) All specifications shall be licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0
b) All reference implementations shall remain under Apache License 2.0
