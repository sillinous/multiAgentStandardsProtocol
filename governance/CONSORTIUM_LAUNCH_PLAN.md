# SuperStandard Consortium Launch Plan

**Version:** 1.0
**Timeline:** 6-Month Launch Roadmap
**Target Launch Date:** TBD + 6 months

---

## Executive Summary

This plan outlines the formation and public launch of the SuperStandard Consortium over a 6-month period. Success metrics: 20+ founding members, $500K+ in commitments, 100+ technical contributors, and significant industry awareness.

**Critical Success Factors:**
- Strong founding member roster (mix of enterprises and startups)
- Technical credibility (working code, proven protocols)
- Clear value proposition
- Professional execution
- Community momentum

## Phase 2: Founding Member Recruitment (Months 2-4)

### Month 2-3: Stealth Outreach

**Strategy:** Private recruitment of founding members before public launch

**Target Profile:**
- **5 Platinum Members** ($50K each = $250K)
- **10 Gold Members** ($25K each = $250K)  
- **5-10 Silver Members** ($10K each = $50-100K)
- **Total:** 20-25 founding members, $550-600K revenue

**Tier 1 Targets (Platinum):**
- Cloud providers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)
- AI platform companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere)
- Enterprise software (Salesforce, Microsoft, Oracle)
- Agent framework companies (LangChain, AutoGPT, etc.)
- Systems integrators (Accenture, Deloitte Digital)

**Tier 2 Targets (Gold):**
- Mid-size AI/ML companies
- Developer tool vendors
- Enterprise adopters (banks, insurance, healthcare)
- Consulting firms
- VCs investing in AI agents

**Tier 3 Targets (Silver):**
- Startups building on multi-agent systems
- Open source projects
- Regional players
- Specialized vendors

**Outreach Process:**

**Week 1-2: Research and Preparation**
- [ ] Build target list (100 companies)
- [ ] Research decision-makers
- [ ] Craft personalized outreach emails
- [ ] Prepare pitch deck
- [ ] Schedule internal pitch practices

**Week 3-4: Initial Outreach**
- [ ] Send personalized emails to top 50 targets
- [ ] Follow-up calls with interested parties
- [ ] Schedule 1:1 pitch meetings
- [ ] Track responses in CRM

**Week 5-8: Pitch Meetings**
- [ ] 30-40 pitch meetings via video
- [ ] Customize value proposition per company
- [ ] Address objections and concerns
- [ ] Provide membership agreements to interested parties
- [ ] Follow up with proposals

**Tools:**
- CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive)
- Pitch deck (see FOUNDING_MEMBERS_PITCH.md)
- Membership agreements
- FAQ document

**Goal:** 10-15 verbal commitments by end of Month 3

### Month 4: Closing Founding Members

**Week 1-2: Commitment Phase**
- [ ] Execute membership agreements
- [ ] Collect first-year dues (50% founding discount)
- [ ] Assign Board seats (Platinum) or class votes (Gold/Silver)
- [ ] Onboard founding members
- [ ] Schedule first Board meeting

**Week 3-4: Working Group Formation**
- [ ] Charter 6 initial working groups
- [ ] Recruit working group chairs from founding members
- [ ] Announce working group formation
- [ ] Schedule first working group meetings
- [ ] Begin technical work on priority protocols

**Milestone:** 20+ founding members committed, $500K+ secured

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Insufficient Founding Members

**Mitigation:**
- Start outreach early (Month 2)
- Over-recruit (target 30 to get 20)
- Flexible tier structure
- Extend founding member period if needed
- Delay launch if < 10 commitments

### Risk 2: Competing Standards

**Mitigation:**
- Emphasize differentiation (blockchain, economics, comprehensiveness)
- Partner with complementary standards (MCP, A2A)
- Focus on gaps competitors don't fill
- Build coalition early

### Risk 3: Technical Credibility Questioned

**Mitigation:**
- Ensure reference implementations are high quality
- Recruit respected technical leaders to TSC
- Publish technical deep-dives
- Demonstrate real-world use cases
- Open source everything

### Risk 4: Budget Overruns

**Mitigation:**
- Conservative budgeting
- Milestone-based spending
- Leverage founding member resources (design, dev, hosting)
- Start lean, scale as revenue grows
- Defer nice-to-haves

### Risk 5: Low Community Engagement

**Mitigation:**
- Active community management from day 1
- Make participation easy and rewarding
- Recognize top contributors
- Host virtual meetups and workshops
- Provide learning resources
